 1. OVERVIEW — WHAT IS IPOS-SA-ACC?
================================================================================

IPOS-SA-ACC is the Account Management package of the IPOS-SA system.  It
handles creating and configuring user accounts, enforcing role-based access
control, and managing the business profile of merchant accounts (contact
details, credit limits, discount plans, and account standing).

The brief defines three types of accounts:

  i)   MERCHANT  — Can access IPOS-SA-CAT (Catalogue) and IPOS-SA-ORD
                    (Orders) only.  Must have contact details, credit limit,
                    and a discount plan configured before the account is
                    activated.

  ii)  ADMIN     — Full access to all packages including IPOS-SA-ACC.

  iii) MANAGER   — Access to IPOS-SA-RPRT (Reporting).  Can also access
                    merchant accounts to alter credit limits, discount plans,
                    and change the state of an "in default" account to either
                    "normal" or "suspended."

-- 2.2  MERCHANT ACCOUNT CREATION (ACC-US1) ---------------------------------

Status: COMPLETE

Brief requirement: "If the required details are not provided the account will
not be created."

Implementation:
  - POST /api/merchant-accounts (ADMIN only) creates a User (role=MERCHANT)
    and a MerchantProfile in a single @Transactional operation.
  - If ANY mandatory field is missing or invalid, the entire operation rolls
    back — no orphan User row can exist without a profile.
  - The generic POST /api/users endpoint REJECTS role=MERCHANT with a clear
    error, forcing all merchant creation through the proper endpoint.

MerchantProfile entity (merchant_profiles table) stores:
  - Contact details:  contactEmail, contactPhone, addressLine (all mandatory)
  - Credit limit:     creditLimit (BigDecimal, must be > 0)
  - Discount plan:    discountPlanType (FIXED or FLEXIBLE)
  - FIXED params:     fixedDiscountPercent (0-100)
  - FLEXIBLE params:  flexibleTiersJson (JSON array of tier definitions)
  - Standing:         NORMAL, IN_DEFAULT, or SUSPENDED (starts as NORMAL)
  - Flexible credit:  flexibleDiscountCredit (accumulated from month-close)
  - Cheque pending:   chequeRebatePending (for cheque settlement mode)

Frontend: MerchantCreate.jsx provides a full form with:
  - Credentials section (name, username, password)
  - Contact details section (email, phone, address)
  - Financial details section (credit limit)
  - Discount plan section with dynamic UI:
    - FIXED: single percentage input
    - FLEXIBLE: tier editor (add/remove tiers with maxExclusive + percent)

Validation is enforced at three levels:
  1. Frontend: HTML5 required attributes + type constraints
  2. DTO: Jakarta Bean Validation (@NotBlank, @NotNull, @DecimalMin)
  3. Service: Business rules (duplicate username, tier JSON structure, plan-
     specific field presence)


-- 2.3  FIXED DISCOUNT PLAN (Brief §i) --------------------------------------

Status: COMPLETE

"The same discount rate applies to all items of any quantity ordered from the
catalogue.  The discount is applied to each order and the amount due is
reduced by the amount of the discount at the time of placing an order."

Implementation in OrderService.placeOrder():
  1. Compute grossTotal = sum of (unitPriceAtOrder * quantity) for all items.
  2. fixedDiscountAmount = grossTotal * (fixedDiscountPercent / 100).
  3. totalDue = grossTotal - fixedDiscountAmount.
  4. All three values are persisted on the Order entity for audit.

Example: Merchant has 5% fixed discount, orders £200 of products.
  grossTotal = £200.00
  fixedDiscountAmount = £10.00
  totalDue = £190.00


-- 2.4  FLEXIBLE DISCOUNT PLAN (Brief §i) -----------------------------------

Status: COMPLETE

"The discount rates depend on the value of all orders within a calendar month.
The amount of the discount is either paid back to the merchant by sending a
cheque at the end of the month or deducted from the value of the next order(s)
by the same merchant."

Implementation:

  At order placement (OrderService.placeOrder()):
  - No percentage discount is applied at placement time.
  - If the merchant has accumulated flexibleDiscountCredit (from prior month
    closes), that credit is consumed: min(credit, grossTotal) is subtracted
    from grossTotal to produce totalDue.
  - The consumed amount is recorded as flexibleCreditApplied on the Order.

  At month-close (MerchantAccountService.closeMonth()):
  - Input: YearMonth (e.g. "2026-03") + SettlementMode (APPLY_CREDIT or CHEQUE).
  - For each FLEXIBLE-plan merchant:
    a) Sum grossTotal of non-cancelled orders placed in that month.
    b) Look up the merchant's flexible tiers JSON.
    c) Find the applicable tier percentage for the monthly gross.
    d) computedDiscount = monthlyGross * (tierPercent / 100).
    e) APPLY_CREDIT mode: add discount to flexibleDiscountCredit on profile.
       CHEQUE mode: add discount to chequeRebatePending on profile.
    f) Record a MonthlyRebateSettlement row (idempotent: merchant + yearMonth
       unique constraint prevents double-close).

  Tier JSON format:
    [{"maxExclusive":1000,"percent":1},
     {"maxExclusive":2000,"percent":2},
     {"percent":3}]
  The last tier has no maxExclusive — it is the catch-all.

Example: Merchant has above tiers, monthly gross = £1,500.
  Applicable tier: 2% (between £1000 and £2000).
  computedDiscount = £1500 * 0.02 = £30.00.
  If APPLY_CREDIT: flexibleDiscountCredit increases by £30.
  Next order of £100: flexibleCreditApplied = £30, totalDue = £70.


-- 2.5  MANAGER MERCHANT MAINTENANCE (Brief §iii) ---------------------------

Status: COMPLETE

"This user can also access the merchant accounts and alter their credit
limits, discount plans and change the state of an 'in default' account to
either 'normal' or 'suspended'."

Implementation:
  - GET /api/merchant-profiles         → List all merchant profiles (MANAGER/ADMIN)
  - GET /api/merchant-profiles/{userId} → Get one profile (MANAGER/ADMIN)
  - PUT /api/merchant-profiles/{userId} → Update profile (MANAGER/ADMIN)
  - POST /api/merchant-profiles/close-month → Month-close settlement (MANAGER/ADMIN)

  Editable fields via PUT:
    - creditLimit (must be > 0)
    - discountPlanType (FIXED or FLEXIBLE, with corresponding params)
    - standing (with transition rules)

  Standing transition rules (enforced in controller):
    - FROM IN_DEFAULT → TO NORMAL     ✓  (Manager restores the account)
    - FROM IN_DEFAULT → TO SUSPENDED  ✓  (Manager suspends instead)
    - Any other transition             ✗  (400 Bad Request)

Frontend: MerchantManagement.jsx provides:
  - Table of all merchants with standing badges (colour-coded)
  - Inline edit mode for credit limit, plan type/params, standing
  - Month-close section with year-month picker and settlement mode selector


-- 2.6  ORDER PIPELINE ENHANCEMENTS -----------------------------------------

Status: COMPLETE

  a) Price snapshots: unitPriceAtOrder is saved on each OrderItem at order
     time, so historical totals remain stable even if catalogue prices change.

  b) Financial fields on Order: placedAt, grossTotal, fixedDiscountAmount,
     flexibleCreditApplied, totalDue — all persisted for audit.

  c) Standing guard: Orders are blocked for merchants whose standing is
     IN_DEFAULT or SUSPENDED.  Clear error message returned.

  d) Credit limit enforcement: Sum of totalDue across non-cancelled orders +
     new order's totalDue must not exceed the merchant's creditLimit.

  e) ORD-US1 (Merchant Isolation): If the authenticated user is a MERCHANT,
     the backend forces merchantId to the caller's own user ID regardless of
     what was submitted.  Merchants cannot place orders for other merchants.

  f) OrderForm.jsx displays the financial breakdown (gross, discounts,
     totalDue) after a successful order placement.

 3. WHAT IS LEFT TO DO (FUTURE WORK)
================================================================================

-- 3.1  ACC-US2: Fixed Discount Plan Assignment (NOT YET STARTED) -----------

The brief implies discount plans could be shared/named entities rather than
inline on each merchant profile.  Currently, the fixed discount percentage
is stored directly on MerchantProfile.  A future iteration could:
  - Create a DiscountPlan entity with named plans (e.g. "Gold 5%", "Silver 3%").
  - Allow admins to define plans, and then assign them to merchants.
  - This would make it easier to change a plan's rate and have it apply to
    all merchants on that plan.

-- 3.2  ACC-US3: Flexible Discount Plan Configuration (NOT YET STARTED) -----

Currently, flexible tiers are stored as raw JSON.  A future iteration could:
  - Create a dedicated UI screen for defining flexible plan templates.
  - Store tiers as a normalised entity (DiscountTier with @OneToMany).
  - Allow admins to create, edit, and preview tier structures.
  - Show merchants which plan they are on and what their current month's
    accrued discount would be.

-- 3.3  ACC-US5: Managing Defaulted Accounts (PARTIALLY DONE) ---------------

What IS done:
  - MerchantStanding enum: NORMAL, IN_DEFAULT, SUSPENDED.
  - Manager can transition IN_DEFAULT → NORMAL or SUSPENDED.
  - Orders are blocked for non-NORMAL merchants.

What is NOT done:
  - Automatic transition TO IN_DEFAULT (e.g. when credit limit is exceeded
    or invoices are overdue) — currently, only manual standing changes exist.
  - Audit log for standing transitions (who changed it, when, why).
  - Notification to the merchant when their account status changes.

-- 3.4  ACC-US6: Managing Accounts (PARTIALLY DONE) -------------------------

What IS done:
  - Manager can edit credit limits, discount plans, and standing.
  - PUT /api/merchant-profiles/{userId} supports partial updates.

What is NOT done:
  - Manager access to view/edit basic merchant contact details.
  - Dedicated merchant "settings" page that a manager uses vs the broader
    account admin page.
  - Richer search/filter on the merchant list (by standing, plan type, etc.).

-- 3.5  Discount Plan Refinements -------------------------------------------

  - The flexible discount "cheque" path only records a ledger amount
    (chequeRebatePending).  No actual payment integration or cheque
    generation exists.  Replace with real accounts-payable workflow when
    financial integration is scoped.
  - The credit limit check is simplistic: sum of all non-cancelled totalDue.
    A real system would subtract payments received, but payment tracking
    (ORD-US6) is out of scope.

-- 3.6  Testing -------------------------------------------------------------

  - Automated JUnit tests have been created (see section 4 below) but cover

    - Controller tests with @WebMvcTest and MockMvc.
